# IDS & IPS

> Estimated Reading Time: 8–10 minutes
> Difficulty: Beginner
> Prerequisites: Firewalls

## Introduction

Intrusion Detection Systems (IDS) and Intrusion Prevention Systems (IPS) monitor network traffic for suspicious activity. While both inspect traffic, they respond differently.

---

## What You'll Learn

- IDS vs IPS
- Detection methods
- Common deployment locations
- Security benefits

---

## Why It Matters

Firewalls control traffic based on rules. IDS and IPS inspect traffic for malicious behavior that may bypass traditional firewall rules.

---

IDS = Detect & Alert

---

## Real-World Example

An IDS alerts analysts when it detects a known malware signature. An IPS can automatically block the connection before it reaches the target.

---

## Cybersecurity Context

SOC analysts rely on IDS alerts during investigations, while IPS solutions actively prevent many network-based attacks.

---

## Interview Questions

- What is the difference between an IDS and an IPS?
- Does an IDS block traffic?
- Why are IDS and IPS often deployed together?

---

## Summary

IDS detects suspicious activity, while IPS detects and actively prevents malicious traffic.
