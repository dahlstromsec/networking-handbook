# Routing

> Estimated Reading Time: 10 minutes
> Difficulty: Beginner
> Prerequisites: Subnetting

## Introduction

Routing is the process of forwarding packets between different networks. Routers examine destination IP addresses and decide where packets should go next.

---

## What You'll Learn

- Default gateway
- Routing tables
- Static vs Dynamic routing
- Packet forwarding

---

## Why It Matters

Without routing, communication would be limited to devices on the same local network.

---

## Main Concept

Packet

↓

Default Gateway

↓

Router

↓

Routing Table

↓

Next Network

↓

Destination

Every router repeats this process until the packet reaches its destination.

---

## Real-World Example

Opening a website sends packets through multiple routers across your ISP and the Internet before reaching the web server.

---

## Cybersecurity Context

Routing knowledge helps troubleshoot connectivity issues, understand packet flow and analyze VPNs and firewall placement.

---

## Interview Questions

## Summary

Routing connects separate networks by forwarding packets based on destination IP addresses.
