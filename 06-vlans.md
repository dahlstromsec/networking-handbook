# VLANs

> Estimated Reading Time: 8–10 minutes
> Difficulty: Beginner
> Prerequisites: Switching

## Introduction

A Virtual Local Area Network (VLAN) logically separates devices into different networks, even when they are connected to the same physical switch.

---

## What You'll Learn

- What a VLAN is
- Why VLANs exist
- Access and trunk ports
- Network segmentation

---

## Why It Matters

VLANs improve security, reduce broadcast traffic and make networks easier to manage.

---

## Main Concept

Without VLANs:

One large network

↓

All devices communicate freely

With VLANs:

Office VLAN

↓

Server VLAN

↓

Guest VLAN

Each network is isolated unless routing is configured.

---

## Real-World Example

A company separates employees, servers and guest Wi-Fi into different VLANs to improve security.

---

## Cybersecurity Context

Network segmentation limits lateral movement after a compromise and is a common defensive strategy.

---

VLANs logically divide networks to improve security, performance and management.
