# Switching

> Estimated Reading Time: 8–10 minutes
> Difficulty: Beginner
> Prerequisites: MAC Addresses

## Introduction

A switch connects devices inside a local network and forwards Ethernet frames to the correct destination.

---

## What You'll Learn

- What a switch does
- MAC address tables
- Broadcast vs unicast traffic
- Why switches improve network performance

---

## Why It Matters

Without switches, devices could not efficiently communicate on modern local networks.

---

## Main Concept

When a frame arrives:

Switch

↓

Reads destination MAC address

↓

Checks MAC address table

↓

Forwards frame to the correct port

---

## Real-World Example

An office switch connects employee computers, printers and servers while keeping communication efficient.

---

## Cybersecurity Context

Security professionals investigate switch behavior when troubleshooting connectivity, VLANs or MAC flooding attacks.

---

## Interview Questions

- What does a switch use to forward traffic?
- What is a MAC address table?
- How is a switch different from a router?

---

## Summary

Switches forward frames using MAC addresses, making communication inside a LAN efficient.
