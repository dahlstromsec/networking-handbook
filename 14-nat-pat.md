# NAT & PAT

> Estimated Reading Time: 8–10 minutes
> Difficulty: Beginner
> Prerequisites: DHCP

## Introduction

Network Address Translation (NAT) allows private IP addresses to communicate with public networks. Port Address Translation (PAT) enables many devices to share one public IP address.

---

## What You'll Learn

- NAT
- PAT
- Public vs Private addressing
- Why translation is necessary

---

## Why It Matters

Most home and business networks use NAT to connect many devices to the Internet using a single public IP.

## Real-World Example

Your home router translates traffic from phones, laptops and smart TVs before sending it to the Internet.

---

## Cybersecurity Context

NAT hides internal addresses but should never be considered a replacement for a firewall.

## Summary

NAT and PAT allow private networks to communicate efficiently with the Internet while conserving IPv4 addresses.
